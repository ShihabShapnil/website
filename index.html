About Me.

Hardware Engineering

From consoles to computers, I grew up around a lot of technology. My biggest hobby was playing video games, primarily computer games. However, moving forward, the demands for games were taxing to outdated computers. And as any aspiring 14-year-old gamer would be, I learned how to build my own computer. With that, the thirst for building and learning continued to grow.

Technology and Software Development

As the end of high school approached, more computers were built and more knowledge was learned. I was finally able to play the video games I wanted but this only opened another path. I wanted to learn how these video games were created. So I dove into the software aspect of technology and learned 2 languages, HTML and CSS.

Stony Brook University

Ah, time for college. The beginning of college was an interesting time because I wasn’t 100 percent sure if I wanted to focus on the hardware or software aspect of computers. I wanted the best of both worlds, so I decided on Computer Engineering. This gave me the freedom to work with hardware and focus on software at the same time. I believe this was one of the best decisions in my college career.
